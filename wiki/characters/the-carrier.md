# The Carrier

**Role:** Installation artist; gray-market operative (light installations for Corvin Field Services)
**Affiliation:** Corvin Field Services (contracted); independent artist
**Appears in:** [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md)
**Relationship:** Son of [The Spy](the-spy.md)

---

## Voice

Register: technical and aesthetic simultaneously — he thinks about the work in both registers without irony. His interiority is more present-tense than his father's; he is in the installation as it is being made. Pride without arrogance. Minimal reflection on what the work is used for.

Signature moves:
- Concrete, sensory attention to physical process (calibrating anchors, eating kebab, the quality of light)
- The old RAV4 as emotional signal — he drives his father's truck model without apparent awareness of what it signals to the reader
- Not asking what the installation is for — this is not ignorance; it is a professional decision

---

## Want / Need / Lie / Ghost

**Want:** To complete the installation correctly. To have done the work.

**Need:** Some form of acknowledgment of the estrangement — its resolution, or its acceptance.

**Lie:** That the work and its deployment are separable; that "he had done it" exhausts his accountability.

**Ghost:** The estrangement from his father — the cause is unspecified. He drives the old truck model, which may be literal (his father's truck) or may be emotional shorthand.

**Confrontation point:** He sees the OBSERVATION EVENT notification. Something noticed him. He looks at it. He decides not to file a report. He doesn't know it was his father.

---

## Arc Summary

The Carrier completes a massive light installation in the Central Asian steppe — four resonance anchors, visual band, sustained light — for Corvin Field Services. He does this with complete attention and real pride. In the middle of the calibration, he sees an OBSERVATION EVENT notification on his system: something recorded his location. He considers it. He doesn't file a report. The lights go on as scheduled. "He had done it." The story ends with him on his tailgate, in his desert, as the installation runs. He does not know his father was on the drone. He does not know what the installation is covering.

---

## Relationships

- **The Spy** — his father; estranged; was on the drone that recorded him; see [The Spy](the-spy.md)
- **Corvin Field Services** — his employer for this contract; uses his installations as reconnaissance cover

---

## Canon Ambiguities

- Whether "the old RAV4" is literally his father's truck or just the same model — the text implies the latter but uses it emotionally as if the former.
- The nature of the estrangement — never specified. Its weight is present throughout but its origin is absent.
- Does he know, at any level, that Corvin uses his installations for reconnaissance? The story implies professional not-asking, but the degree of awareness is ambiguous.
