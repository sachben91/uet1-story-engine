# Rowan

**Role:** Somatic self-optimization specialist; gray-market peptide courier
**Affiliation:** Independent; recruited by Marcus Hale operatives for the Stolar extraction
**Appears in:** [The Chartered Flesh](../stories/the-chartered-flesh.md)

---

## Voice

Register: sardonic, technical, working-class Midlands British. She uses the vocabulary of somatic optimization fluently — not to signal expertise but because it is how she thinks. Her humor is dry and deflating. She does not explain herself.

Signature moves:
- Precision technical language for the body alongside casual dismissiveness for everything else
- Observation without narration: she notes things without concluding from them (until the end)
- Understatement in the face of significant events

---

## Want / Need / Lie / Ghost

**Want:** To deliver the compound, get paid, and continue her own somatic program on better terms.

**Need:** To maintain sovereignty over her body — to be the person who optimizes it rather than the material being optimized.

**Lie:** That the body she has perfected is her own and acts on her instructions alone.

**Ghost:** Unspecified; implied in her investment in somatic autonomy — something drove her to make the body the site of her self-determination.

**Confrontation point:** The Car 7 passage. She does not know it is happening. The confrontation is invisible — which is the story's argument. Her body was read and used without her awareness or consent. She receives the "Class 2 metabolic variance" notification and cannot decode it.

---

## Arc Summary

Rowan accepts the job as a professional courier transaction. She administers BRX-90 to herself and four friends, believing it is a metabolic-enhancement compound. She is wrong. In Car 7, the decontamination array reads the encoded cryptographic keys from their bodies; a compromised relay routes the data to Hale's operatives in Zug. Rowan notices something anomalous in her biometric notification but cannot interpret it. At severance, Alex gives her 30 vials of "the real compound" — the masking agent she thought she was carrying. "This one actually works." She understands, retrospectively, that what she delivered was not what she thought. Whether she understands the full scope — that her body carried the Stolar keys — is ambiguous. The recurring dreams about Car 7, "being read as a surface," suggest the body absorbed what the mind hasn't yet processed.
