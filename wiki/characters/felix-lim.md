# Felix Lim

**Role:** Chief of Staff to Lin Yuan Exact; Neutral Forge
**Age:** 26 at story time; nine months in the role
**Affiliation:** Neutral Forge / Exact Yield Semiconductors; becomes an agent of the Covenant by story's end
**Appears in:** [The Headless Empire](../stories/the-headless-empire.md)

---

## Voice

Register: formal, analytical, institutional. His professionalism is sincere, not performed. In his own head, he is somewhat plainer — the analytic habit persists but the sentences are shorter. He constructs arguments. He applies frameworks. He reaches conclusions.

The key: **his conclusions are slightly wrong in a way he cannot see.** He has the right variables and the wrong model. This is visible to the reader simultaneously with the correctness of his reasoning.

Signature moves:
- "The arc of innovation bends toward justice" — his foundational thesis, stated with full sincerity, tested throughout, replaced by its inverse
- Framework application: he identifies variables correctly and models them incorrectly
- The thesis as reflex — by Sections VI–VIII he notices he is reaching for it automatically, a beat before it lands
- Absence: in the final section, he has nothing where the thesis used to be. Then something else.

**Arc progression of internal voice:**
- Sections I–IV: thesis stated with full confidence
- Section V: thesis reached for, catches slightly, restated
- Sections VI–VIII: thesis becomes a reflex; he notices it
- Sections IX–X: he stops reaching for it; the absence is louder than the thesis was
- Section XI: something else is there instead; he does not name it yet

## Want / Need / Lie / Ghost

**Want:** Scene-by-scene:
- I: Confirmation the system works as described
- II: Nothing from Lin — he is exploring, cataloguing
- III: To understand what pressure Lin is under, in terms he was taught
- IV: To perform competence; to be trusted; to be in the room
- V: To be told his framework still applies (the last time he asks)
- VI: He is not searching anymore — he receives
- VII: To act correctly; his last institutional act
- VIII: To have a conversation; there is no one to have it with
- IX: To locate responsibility; to find the appeals process
- X: To understand what his report actually did
- XI: Something he cannot name yet; possibly to hear that it mattered

**Need:** To know the arc of technology bends toward justice — that what he is doing is in service of something real.

**Lie:** That good-faith institutional action within a well-designed system produces just outcomes.

**Ghost:** The education — "the finest university in the world" — that gave him the frameworks. The frameworks are not wrong. The world does not operate on them.

**Confrontation point:** Section VI — he overhears the Covenant activation. He is not searching anymore. He receives the truth without asking for it.

## Arc Summary

Felix boards the UET-1 as Lin's Chief of Staff, believing the Forge's mission is genuinely emancipatory. He watches Lin, delivers a folder at Lin's instruction, files an institutional report at 2:47am — his most reasonable act, his purest act. In Lisbon, he learns the report was used as a trade chip. Lin has already disappeared. Felix walks the city. He returns south on the train as an agent of the Covenant — not because he was recruited, but because the logic converted him. The arc of innovation does not bend toward justice. It bends toward capture. He has upgraded his faith, not lost it. On the final call, Lin and Felix are on opposite sides of the irony — Lin in a wadi, unsettled about whether he did the right thing; Felix on the train, more certain than Lin ever was. Neither knows the other's position.
