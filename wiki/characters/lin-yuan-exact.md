# Lin Yuan Exact

**Role:** CEO of the Neutral Forge (Exact Yield Semiconductors); builder of the Covenant
**Affiliation:** Neutral Forge; no empire
**Appears in:** [The Headless Empire](../stories/the-headless-empire.md); referenced in world documentation
**Current status:** In Saudi Arabia (location of wadi; see Ambiguities)

---

## Voice

Register: aphoristic, minimal, self-contained. He speaks as though reading from a manual he wrote himself. He gives Felix the minimum viable information — until he doesn't. In Section III (Mongolia), the steppe loosens something; he explains more than intended. In Section V (dining car), he gives Felix everything. These are deviations from his baseline, not the baseline itself.

Signature moves:
- Statements framed as engineering observations about structure: "The contract that requires a human to enforce it has already failed"
- Resuming what he was doing after speaking — eating with complete attention, looking out windows
- Physical presence as part of voice: a pause where he looks at the goats is not stage direction; it is the answer
- What Lin does not say is often more significant than what he does

Lin's physical presence is information. He eats with complete attention. He resumes what he was doing. These actions are not decorative.

## Want / Need / Lie / Ghost

**Want:** Scene-by-scene:
- I: To get to Lisbon without incident; Felix is useful, not important
- III: The steppe loosened something; he explains more than intended; testing whether Felix can receive real information
- IV: To use Felix cleanly; the explanation he gives Felix is also Lin rationalising the use to himself
- V: He gives Felix everything — because Felix is about to be used in a final way and Lin owes him the truth. Or: Lin needs someone to have heard it.
- VI: Lin does not know Felix is in the corridor
- XI: He needs this to have been real. "I built something that cannot be challenged." Felix is the witness.

**Need:** Escape from persecution by both empires without paying the cost. A free lunch. He does not believe in free lunches. He engineers one anyway.

**Lie:** That escape and consequence are separable — that he can disappear cleanly, the machine still running, hands unbloodied.

**Ghost:** The years spent building something unchallengeable; the knowledge that being challengeable was always the threat; the free lunch as the thing he knew couldn't exist but built toward anyway.

**Confrontation point:** Section V — he gives Felix the world machine speech. This is Lin at maximum exposure. He is not teaching. He is making a case, the jury himself.

## Arc Summary

Lin boards the UET-1 knowing he is walking through a gap before it closes. Both empires want him for different reasons; the Forge's neutrality has made him simultaneously invaluable and threatened. He uses Felix as an instrument throughout — the folder delivery, the good-faith report — while also, in his more unguarded moments, explaining the architecture of what he built. He disappears before Lisbon. The Covenant runs. Felix finds two coffees. In the final call from the wadi, Lin needs to be told it was real — "I built something that cannot be challenged." Felix, now a true believer, confirms this. Lin hangs up before Felix can say anything else. He is settled, not triumphant. He has accepted the cost.

## Canon Ambiguities

**Lin's fate — major ambiguity:**

*The story (v1–v6 and the headless-empire-rewrite skill)* presents Lin as having chosen his situation — philosophical, settled, a goat farmer who accepts the cost of what he built.

*The UET-1 Wiki PDF* states: Lin's original plan was extraction to Geneva for asylum and quiet retirement. His operatives accepted a better offer and delivered him to Saudi Arabia instead. He "expressed no distress."

These are not incompatible — Lin may have accepted a situation he did not choose — but the voluntary/involuntary question changes the story's meaning significantly. If he chose Saudi Arabia, he paid the cost and accepted it. If he was delivered there, he is still paying the cost but with less agency than the story implies.

The "expressed no distress" may be performance, acceptance, or both.

**This ambiguity may be intentional.** Lin is unreliable as a narrator of his own fate. The wiki cannot resolve it. Both versions are recorded here.
