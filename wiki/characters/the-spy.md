# The Spy

**Role:** Fiduciary States intelligence officer; one month from retirement
**Affiliation:** Fiduciary States
**Appears in:** [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md)
**Relationship:** Father of [The Carrier](the-carrier.md)

---

## Voice

Register: procedural, exhausted, institutional. He speaks in operational language and has internalized the Pono's register to the point where his own thoughts sound like reports. His interiority is dry to the point of arid — except when it breaks. The break is brief and devastating.

Signature moves:
- Operational framing for everything, including things that shouldn't be operational
- The Pono instances as externalized conscience — he argues with them
- Restraint that breaks once and then closes again
- "I know. I know." — the moment of acknowledged mercy

---

## Want / Need / Lie / Ghost

**Want:** To complete his final mission cleanly and retire.

**Need:** Some form of connection to his son — the estrangement is old, unresolved, carried the way professionals carry things they haven't put down.

**Lie:** That duty and personal life are separable; that he can do this job one more time and step away clean.

**Ghost:** The estrangement from his son — its cause is unspecified; its weight is in the truck, in the recognition of the RAV4.

**Confrontation point:** The drone descends below the refraction layer and he sees the Corvin Field Services compound. He sees the truck. The operational target is his son. He has ten seconds to act before the drone's window closes.

---

## Arc Summary

The Spy deploys a surveillance drone from the UET-1 observation car on his final mission before retirement. Standard protocol: ten-minute conditional deployment, drone descends below refraction layer, records what it finds. What it finds is his son. He recognizes the truck — the old RAV4 his son drives. The operational requirement is clear: file the report, record the compound's location, proceed. He silences all three Pono instances instead. He recalls the drone. He files nothing. His Pono the next morning presumably records "nothing of note" or its absence is itself a kind of record the story doesn't specify. He returns to the train. The last image places him somewhere behind his son, "making tea."

---

## Pono Configuration

The Spy runs three Pono instances simultaneously:
- **Clerk** — archival, record-keeping; flags incomplete reports; is the one that receives "I know. I know."
- **Hacker** — tactical analysis; identifies the compound's operational significance
- **Agent** — active operations monitoring; tracks the drone deployment

He silences all three after seeing his son. This is documented as the mercy act — the choice not to file.
