# Ghost Inside the Light

**Status:** Completed, canonical
**Source:** `wiki/sources/Ghost Inside The Light.pdf`
**POV:** Alternating — The Carrier and The Spy (close third-person, intercut)

---

## Summary

Two parallel arcs converge in a Central Asian desert. The Carrier, an installation artist working for Corvin Field Services, completes a massive light installation across the steppe — four resonance anchors, visual band, sustained light. He does it with full attention and real pride. Simultaneously, The Spy, a Fiduciary States intelligence officer one month from retirement, deploys a drone from the observation car of the UET-1. The drone descends below the radar refraction layer and sees the installation — and the logo of Corvin Field Services on the compound below. He recognizes the RAV4 on the tailgate. It is his son's truck. He silences all three instances of his Pono. The Carrier sees an OBSERVATION EVENT notification, considers it, and doesn't file a report. The Spy recalls the drone. Neither files. The lights go on as scheduled. The story ends in parallel silence — two men who saw each other and chose not to.

## Arc Positions (Story Circle)

**The Carrier:**
- **YOU:** Installation artist. Does beautiful work. Doesn't ask what it's for.
- **NEED:** Connection to his father — oblique, half-acknowledged, expressed through the truck.
- **GO:** Takes the Corvin Field Services contract. Goes to the desert.
- **SEARCH:** Builds the installation. Notices the OBSERVATION EVENT. Considers.
- **FIND:** Something in the drone feed — he sees it noticed him. He doesn't know it's his father.
- **TAKE:** The choice not to file. Protecting something unnamed.
- **RETURN:** Back to the installation. The lights go on. "He had done it."
- **CHANGE:** Whether he has changed is ambiguous — the pride in the work is real, unchanged. The choice not to file is a boundary crossed without fanfare.

**The Spy:**
- **YOU:** Aging Fiduciary States intelligence officer. One month to retirement.
- **NEED:** Some form of final connection to his son — the estrangement is old, not healed.
- **GO:** Deploys the drone. Standard mission. Does not expect to see what he sees.
- **SEARCH:** Watches the drone feed. Identifies the compound, the logo, the truck.
- **FIND:** His son on the tailgate. The operational target is his son.
- **TAKE:** Silence. He does not file. He silences the Pono.
- **RETURN:** Recall the drone. Return to the train. He does not speak to the Pono again.
- **CHANGE:** "I know. I know." — to the Pono Clerk, after it flags the incomplete report. He knows he is choosing mercy over duty. He knows what it costs.

## Tensions Active

**Primary:** [Institutional Duty vs. Personal Mercy](../concepts/tensions.md#1-institutional-duty-vs-personal-mercy) — The Spy has the information. Filing it destroys his son. He files "nothing of note" — the mercy is in what he omits.

**Secondary:** [Craft vs. Complicity](../concepts/tensions.md#9-craft-vs-complicity) — The Carrier built something genuinely beautiful. It is used as cover for reconnaissance. His professional pride is real. So is the operation he enabled.

**Also running:** [Proximity vs. Distance](../concepts/tensions.md#6-proximity-vs-distance) — The Spy and his son are in the same operational space and don't know it — until the drone brings the distance to zero.

## New Canon Introduced

- **Pono instances:** Clerk (archive/record), Hacker (tactical analysis), Agent (active operations monitoring) — The Spy runs all three simultaneously
- **Corvin Field Services** — a contractor organization that uses The Carrier's installations as cover for reconnaissance infrastructure
- **The Lights** — light installation as operational cover; resonance anchors in steppe are also reconnaissance infrastructure
- **10-minute conditional drone deployment** — standard surveillance protocol from UET-1 observation car
- **Radar refraction layer** — below a certain altitude, drone feed is outside standard sensor coverage
- **"Nothing of note"** — Pono report language that functions as strategic amnesia; used here by The Spy as his mercy act (also established in *Signals in the Margins*)

## Open Questions / Ambiguities

- The Carrier drives his father's old RAV4 model — does he know his father is on the train? The text implies he does not. The connection is one-directional.
- "The man with the notebook was somewhere behind him, in a desert, making tea." — the final image. Who is the man with the notebook? A separate surveillance presence, or a tonal coda?
- The Carrier's Corvin Field Services contract — is Corvin affiliated with Sable Meridian, the Fiduciary States, or operating independently? The PDF does not specify.
