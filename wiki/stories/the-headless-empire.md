## Summary

Felix Lim, 26-year-old Chief of Staff to Lin Yuan Exact, CEO of the Neutral Forge, boards the UET-1 from Hong Kong to Lisbon for a summit that is officially about peace and unofficially about Lin's disappearance from the board of two competing empires. Over three days, Felix watches Lin, delivers a folder at Lin's instruction, overhears the mechanics of the Covenant being activated in the next compartment, files a good-faith institutional report at 2:47am, and discovers in Lisbon that his report was used as a trade chip in negotiations he didn't know were happening. Lin disappears. Felix returns south on the train, now a true believer in the Covenant's logic — more completely than Lin ever was. Lin calls from a wadi in Saudi Arabia. The call ends before Felix can respond.

## Arc Positions (Story Circle)

**Felix Lim:**
- **YOU:** 26, Chief of Staff, nine months in role. Believes the arc of technology bends toward justice. Intelligence real. Frameworks correct. The world does not operate on his frameworks.
- **NEED:** Confirmation that what he's doing is in service of something real.
- **GO:** To Lisbon. On the train.
- **SEARCH:** Watching Lin, studying the doctrine, delivering the folder, filing the report — each is a test of his belief. He thinks he is doing his job.
- **FIND:** Lin managed him as an instrument. The Covenant removes accountability by design. His report was used as a trade chip. The arc bends toward capture.
- **TAKE:** Cynicism not as despair, but as clarity. A harder material.
- **RETURN:** Back to work. South from Hong Kong on the train. As an agent of the Covenant.
- **CHANGE:** He now believes in the machine's sovereignty more completely than Lin does. He did not lose his faith. He upgraded it.

**Lin Yuan Exact:**
- **YOU:** CEO of the Neutral Forge. Built something unchallengeable. Is himself challengeable — by both empires simultaneously.
- **NEED:** Escape from persecution without paying the cost of escape. A free lunch. He does not believe in free lunches.
- **GO:** To Lisbon. Walking through the gap before it closes.
- **SEARCH:** For absolution. He thinks he is executing a plan. Every time he explains something to Felix, he is making a case to himself.
- **FIND:** No free lunches. The Covenant runs. Escape has a cost. What he was trying to protect — his ability to be in the world, consequentially — is what he had to give up.
- **TAKE:** The belief that the world is indifferent to man's best-laid plans.
- **RETURN:** The call to Felix. He returns once because Felix is the only witness.
- **CHANGE:** Commits to the ascetic life. Not triumphant. Settled.

## Tensions Active

**Primary:** [System vs. Person](../concepts/tensions.md#4-system-vs-person) — Lin built the Covenant to replace human enforcement with self-executing architecture. Felix believed this was emancipatory. Both discover the system is indifferent to the difference between liberation and capture.

**Secondary:** [Infrastructure as Liberation vs. Capture](../concepts/tensions.md#5-infrastructure-as-liberation-vs-infrastructure-as-capture) — The Covenant was built to make contracts autonomous and fair. It now runs without human oversight in ways Lin may not have intended.

**Also running:** [Craft vs. Complicity](../concepts/tensions.md#9-craft-vs-complicity) — Felix's expertise in good-faith institutional work is what makes him useful as an instrument. His competence enables the operation.

## Open Questions / Ambiguities

**Lin's fate — major ambiguity:** The story presents Lin as settled and philosophical in a Saudi wadi. The UET-1 Wiki PDF states his extraction was planned for Geneva but operatives "accepted a better offer" and delivered him to Saudi Arabia. He "expressed no distress" — which could mean acceptance or performance. Whether Lin chose Saudi Arabia or was betrayed there is unresolved. The story treats it as chosen; the wiki document implies it was not. This ambiguity may be intentional — Lin is unreliable even as a narrator of his own fate.

- What does Felix say after Lin hangs up? The story withholds this deliberately.
- Felix "returned south on the train as an agent of the Covenant" — what does this mean operationally? Does he have a new role, or has he simply adopted the belief system?
- The Strait Country man in Lisbon who tells Felix what his report did — who is this person, and how do they know?
