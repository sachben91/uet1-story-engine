# Signals in the Margins

**Status:** Completed, canonical
**Source:** `wiki/sources/Signals in the Margins.pdf`
**POV:** Detective Pavel Murkin (close third-person)

---

## Summary

Detective Pavel Murkin is assigned a Pono unit at a Russian border junction on the UET-1 — "volunteered" in the bureaucratic sense, which means he had no choice. Out of boredom, he opens the archive and finds a missing person file for Stepan, a border runner who disappeared into fog near Irkutsk. The file contains 14,326 documents — and within them, soup recipes. Shchi, pelmeni, stewed carp. With margin symbols: a triangle of dots, wavy lines, an imperfect circle. The next day, a refugee boy appears at a siding hut with a triangle-of-dots card. The Pono explains: Mirage Handler is a Cold War routing protocol repurposed by someone to give refugees invisible passage. Stepan built this. The soup recipes are decoy filler; the symbols route people through gaps in the surveillance grid. Murkin does not report the boy. His Pono report the next morning reads: "nothing of note: routine drizzle, minor delays, no irregularities." The boy is gone.

## Arc Positions (Story Circle)

**Murkin:**
- **YOU:** Deadpan proceduralist. Assigned a Pono he didn't request. Does his job correctly and as little as possible.
- **NEED:** Something he can't name — a reason to care, perhaps, or a way to act within an institution that has made care difficult.
- **GO:** Opens the archive. He doesn't have to — he does it out of boredom.
- **SEARCH:** Reads 14,326 documents. Follows the soup recipes. Finds the margin symbols.
- **FIND:** The system Stepan built. The boy at the siding hut. The thing that is happening right now.
- **TAKE:** The knowledge that the archive contains an active mercy operation. And a choice.
- **RETURN:** He files his report.
- **CHANGE:** He uses the Pono's language against its institutional purpose: "nothing of note" as a moral act. He does not stop being a proceduralist. He becomes a different kind of one.

## Tensions Active

**Primary:** [Institutional Duty vs. Personal Mercy](../concepts/tensions.md#1-institutional-duty-vs-personal-mercy) — The complete version: Murkin has the information, has the institutional tool, and chooses amnesia. The story's argument is that strategic amnesia is the dominant moral act when the institution's power exceeds its justice.

**Secondary:** [Memory vs. Amnesia](../concepts/tensions.md#7-memory-vs-amnesia) — Mirage Handler itself is institutional amnesia repurposed for survival. Murkin's "nothing of note" joins the archive of strategic forgetting.

**Also running:** [Visibility vs. Erasure](../concepts/tensions.md#2-visibility-vs-erasure) — The refugee boy exists in the system only insofar as no one files him.

## New Canon Introduced

- **[Mirage Handler](../world/mirage-handler.md)** — Cold War routing protocol; soup recipes replaced classified files for backward compatibility; margin symbols (triangle of dots, wavy lines, imperfect circle) used as routing codes; Stepan repurposed it for refugee movement
- **Stepan** — border runner, disappeared, built a refugee network inside institutional amnesia. 14,326 documents. "Fog, Irkutsk sector."
- **"Nothing of note"** — Pono report language as moral act; first explicit theorization (also appears in *Ghost Inside the Light*)
- **Pono "order of metaphysical improbability"** — archival browsing option; the Pono has opinions about what matters
- **47 vendor libraries and 12 ministerial approvals** — what it would take to remove Mirage Handler from the system. It stays because removal is harder than ignoring it.

## Open Questions / Ambiguities

- What happened to Stepan? His disappearance is the catalyst, but the story ends without resolving it.
- Is the triangle-of-dots / wavy lines / imperfect circle symbol system still active elsewhere on the UET-1? The story implies it is — the boy knew to use it.
- The Pono's "order of metaphysical improbability" — is this a documented feature, or something specific to Murkin's unit?
