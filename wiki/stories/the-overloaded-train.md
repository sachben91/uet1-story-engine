# The Overloaded Train

**Status:** Completed, canonical
**Source:** Protocolized (Substack), May 22, 2026. Co-authored by Sachin Benny and Spencer Nitkey.
**POV:** Fu Kenan (close third-person)
**Note:** First crossover between the UET-1 universe and Spencer Nitkey's Zoothesia universe.

---

## Summary

Fu Kenan, 23, junior corridor attendant assigned to Car 16, has spent seven months walking 184 paces from vestibule to vestibule, sixty times per shift, three shifts per run. To keep himself present, he invents a game: guess which passenger left a trace — a smear, a spill — before his overlay identifies it. The game is private, unrecorded, his. Then, hungover after the Almaty gauge change, he places characters from a children's novel at points along the corridor as a memory exercise — a technique his grandmother taught him, which she learned surviving a Qinghai camp. The Zoo's classifier, encountering the memory palace images as objects with too many contradictory readings, begins erasing the corresponding surfaces from his perception. Fu notices. He intensifies the method deliberately, installing increasingly vivid and grotesque images at every locus in the car, until Car 16 disappears around him surface by surface: walls, lighting, doors, passengers. When the classifier's suppression layer finally collapses, exhausted, three hidden figures become visible — a young man, a woman, a child — all marked with the Mirage Handler symbols Murkin had found at the border junction. They had been rendered invisible by the Zoothesia Protocols before his shift began. Fu Kenan looks at them. He does not raise the alarm.

## Arc Positions (Story Circle)

**Fu Kenan:**
- **YOU:** Junior corridor attendant, seven months into a posting that is dissolving him by repetition. He has calculated that by year's end he will have walked one and a half orbits of the Earth, entirely within an 18-metre tube.
- **NEED:** To be seen; or, failing that, to find a way to survive the repetition without disappearing entirely.
- **GO:** Invents the guessing game. Then, accidentally, the memory palace.
- **SEARCH:** Installs images, watches the classifier erase each locus, intensifies. The corridor vanishes around him surface by surface.
- **FIND:** Three people, marked with Mirage Handler symbols, standing in the nothing Car 16 has become. The suppression layer the Protocols had been maintaining around them has finally collapsed.
- **TAKE:** Understanding: they were erased by the same mechanism that erased his brass fittings. And he caused the suppression to fail.
- **RETURN:** Stands at the vestibule door. Does not raise the alarm.
- **CHANGE:** He was becoming invisible by attrition. He has chosen to see — and protect — others who were made invisible by design. The technique his grandmother used to survive erasure has been used, inverted, to break it.

## Tensions Active

**Primary:** [Visibility vs. Erasure](../concepts/tensions.md#2-visibility-vs-erasure) — The Zoothesia Protocols had rendered the Mirage Handler refugees imperceptible before Fu's shift began. His memory palace destroyed the suppression layer that was maintaining their invisibility. The story is about seeing and not-seeing at every level: the game, the grandmother, the Zoo, the hidden passengers, Fu's own slow disappearance.

**Secondary:** [Institutional Duty vs. Personal Mercy](../concepts/tensions.md#1-institutional-duty-vs-personal-mercy) — Fu understands what he has found. He does not raise the alarm. Like Murkin's "nothing of note," his mercy is enacted through inaction inside an institutional role.

**Also running:** [Craft vs. Complicity](../concepts/tensions.md#9-craft-vs-complicity) — His grandmother's memory technique, developed for survival under erasure, becomes the instrument that reveals people being erased. The craft is not complicit — but it produces consequences the craftsman did not plan for.

## New Canon Introduced

- **Fu Kenan** — junior corridor attendant, Car 16, seven months posted, age 23. The only staff member in this story. No prior appearance.
- **The fenestra** — perceptual implant issued to all UET-1 staff, seated at the base of the skull, calibrated at Kowloon depot to the attendant profile. Unlike the passenger lens disc (disposable, fitted at boarding), the fenestra is permanent and non-removable. Staff have no control over their overlay settings.
- **Passenger lens disc** — disposable perceptual overlay issued at boarding. Delivers the Zoo's corridor enhancements: invented depth cues, motion dampening, simulated light cycles, parallax. Distinct from the staff fenestra.
- **The Zoo's passenger enhancements** — corridor windows retouched with invented foothills and rivers; carriage lurches visually dampened; repetitive corridor geometry overlaid with soft parallax; ambient light cycles simulating morning and evening independent of actual longitude.
- **The classifier / perceptual guarantor** — the Zoo's per-observer parser. Maintains a small compute window and decides what a given observer can safely be allowed to see. Objects with too many simultaneous contradictory readings overload the window; the classifier resolves the ambiguity by erasing the object rather than spending further cycles. This is documented in the Zoo protocol leaflets at the Kowloon depot.
- **The memory palace as classifier exploit** — vivid images installed at physical loci via the grandmother's technique read, to the classifier, as objects with irresolvable density of meaning. Sufficient installation saturates the classifier's available compute and causes the suppression layer to fail.
- **Mirage Handler refugees on the train** — the hidden passengers in Car 16 are marked with the Mirage Handler symbols (triangle of dots, wavy lines, imperfect circles). They had been rendered invisible by the Zoothesia Protocols before Fu's shift began. The Zone's classifier had erased them at the protocol level. Their presence in Car 16 is not accidental — they knew the car's physical geometry without needing to see it.
- **Zoothesia Protocols as a hiding mechanism** — the Mirage Handler network is actively exploiting the Zoothesia classifier. Bodies marked with sufficient symbol density create the same irresolvable reading as a faceted object or painted corpse. The Zone's suppression layer, designed to render the steppe's unregistered populations invisible, has been turned against itself to move people through the train unseen.
- **ZOOTHESIA OPERATIONAL ZONE / NF PROTOCOL AUTHORITY** — the zone entry marker appears in the corridor strip at 04:17 during the Central Asian crossing. The zone authority is the Neutral Forge Protocol, not UET-1 or any jurisdictional authority.
- **Fu's grandmother's Qinghai camp** — the memory palace technique was taught among women in the barracks during long winter nights: place vivid images in imagined rooms, walk through them nightly to hold the faces of the people you intend to come back to. His grandmother survived. She taught Fu at nine years old at her kitchen table in Lanzhou. He had forgotten it until Car 16.

## Open Questions / Ambiguities

- How long have Mirage Handler refugees been exploiting the Zoothesia classifier? The story implies this is an established practice — the passengers knew the car's geometry without vision. Stepan may have designed the symbol density specifically to trigger the classifier.
- Does the Zoo know? The Neutral Forge Protocol Authority governs the zone. Whether NF is aware the classifier is being exploited as a hiding mechanism is not addressed.
- What happens to Fu Kenan after Moscow? "The cloaked riders will know where to go when the zone ends. He will follow them." This suggests active complicity going forward — but what form it takes is not stated.
- Is the fenestra standard across all UET-1 staff routes, or only for crews operating through Zoo zones?
- The Portuguese nun: she is the only passenger who acknowledged Fu's existence. He installs her nod on her, and she vanishes. The story does not resolve whether this is a loss or a gift.
- The Zoothesia universe origin: this story is a crossover with Spencer Nitkey's Zoothesia series. The full mechanics of the Zoothesia Protocols, the Neutral Forge Protocol Authority, and the Zoo's governance structure are developed in that series and not fully elaborated here.
