# Tensions

**Category:** Structural Concept
**Source:** `skills/story-premise/references/tensions.md`

A tension is more than a trade-off. It is a trade-off where different actors have conflicting preferences about how the trade-off should be made — and that conflict is ongoing, structural, and unresolvable. Tensions are not problems to be solved. They are the conditions under which UET-1 stories happen.

---

## The Nine Tensions

### 1. Institutional Duty vs. Personal Mercy

The system functions through complete and accurate reporting. Incomplete reports corrupt institutional reliability. But accurate reports destroy specific people.

**Poles:** Duty (the Pono, ECOROUTE, Captain Eriksson's doctrine) vs. Mercy (Murkin, The Spy, The Carrier — each chooses not to file, each for different reasons)

**Active in:** [Signals in the Margins](../stories/signals-in-the-margins.md) (primary), [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md) (primary)

**Pairs well with:** Memory vs. Amnesia; Body as Instrument vs. Self

---

### 2. Visibility vs. Erasure

Biometric identity is legal identity. To exist in the system, you must be seen. But to survive certain things, you must become invisible.

**Poles:** Visibility (ECOROUTE, bio-bands, Pono, Three Oracles) vs. Erasure (Mirage Handler, Shunning, refugees, gray market operatives)

**Active in:** [Signals in the Margins](../stories/signals-in-the-margins.md), [The Chartered Flesh](../stories/the-chartered-flesh.md)

**Note:** A black bio-band does not mean dead — it means non-person. The person still exists; the system has decided not to see them.

---

### 3. Body as Instrument vs. Body as Self

The body can be optimized and made more capable — but optimization opens it to instrumentalization. The more the body is made into an asset, the more useful it becomes to others.

**Poles:** Self (Rowan's self-optimization program, somatic sovereignty argument) vs. Instrument (BRX-90, ECOROUTE biometric surveillance, Stolar Key Extraction)

**Active in:** [The Chartered Flesh](../stories/the-chartered-flesh.md) (primary)

**The trap:** The operation that targets you is only possible because you made your body so legible.

---

### 4. System vs. Person

A system that requires human judgment is corruptible. A system that runs without humans is impartial — but cannot distinguish mercy from harm.

**Poles:** System (the Covenant, ECOROUTE, Captain Eriksson's doctrine) vs. Person (Felix's belief, Murkin's mercy, The Spy's one act of fatherhood)

**Active in:** [The Headless Empire](../stories/the-headless-empire.md) (primary), ambient in every story

**Lin's maxim:** "The contract that requires a human to enforce it has already failed" — which becomes more sinister after he disappears.

---

### 5. Infrastructure as Liberation vs. Infrastructure as Capture

Infrastructure opens up movement, commerce, connection. But whoever builds the rails also determines who can use them, under what conditions, and what data flows through them.

**Poles:** Liberation (the original UET-1 promise, the Covenant's stated purpose, The Lights as genuine aesthetic achievement) vs. Capture (ECOROUTE after Black Wednesday, the Covenant running autonomously, The Lights as reconnaissance infrastructure)

**Active in:** [The Headless Empire](../stories/the-headless-empire.md), [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md)

**The pattern:** Every infrastructure layer has a surveillance layer underneath it.

---

### 6. Proximity vs. Distance

Distance from a thing should provide insulation. On UET-1, it doesn't. 23,300km is one long room.

**Poles:** Distance (the belief that moving far enough constitutes escape) vs. Proximity (The Spy finding his son at the end of a drone feed; Lin's wadi phone call)

**Active in:** [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md) (primary)

**The rule:** "Distance is not safety. Distance is inventory reduction."

---

### 7. Memory vs. Amnesia

Institutional memory enables accountability. It also enables persecution. Forgetting is protective. It is also how impunity is maintained.

**Poles:** Memory (the Pono, ECOROUTE retroactive scoring, the Mirage archive) vs. Amnesia (Murkin's "nothing of note," Mirage files replaced with recipes, Lin's clean cabin)

**Active in:** [Signals in the Margins](../stories/signals-in-the-margins.md) (primary), [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md)

**The paradox:** What is in the archive can be used against you. What isn't in the archive doesn't exist — which can save you or erase you.

---

### 8. Gray Market vs. Formal System

Formal systems provide accountability, protection, and legitimate commerce — but they exclude and surveil. Gray markets fill every gap the formal market leaves — but also exploit the unprotected.

**Poles:** Gray (Rowan's courier trade, The Carrier's deniable contracts, Mirage Handler refugee networks) vs. Formal (ECOROUTE, bio-band compliance, the Covenant's published keys)

**Active in:** [The Chartered Flesh](../stories/the-chartered-flesh.md), [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md)

**The problem:** The same gray market infrastructure that allows Rowan to trade peptides allows Hale to deploy operatives.

---

### 9. Craft vs. Complicity

The work is the work. The deployment is something else. Separating these is a professional survival mechanism. But the skill that enables the craft also enables the operation.

**Poles:** Craft (The Lights as genuine aesthetic achievement, Rowan's somatic expertise, Felix's genuine belief) vs. Complicity (the reconnaissance The Lights cover, the BRX-90 in Rowan's blood, the Covenant's capture function)

**Active in:** [Ghost Inside the Light](../stories/ghost-inside-the-light.md) (primary), [The Chartered Flesh](../stories/the-chartered-flesh.md)

**The rule:** Every skilled person on UET-1 is being used more completely than they understand.

## Tension Nesting Patterns (tested combinations)

| Primary | Secondary | What it produces |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Institutional Duty vs. Mercy | Memory vs. Amnesia | A character who can only show mercy through what they refuse to document |
| Body as Instrument vs. Self | Visibility vs. Erasure | A character whose optimization made them legible; legibility made them a target |
| System vs. Person | Infrastructure as Liberation vs. Capture | A character who built the system and now cannot escape it |
| Proximity vs. Distance | Craft vs. Complicity | Two people whose estrangement is mediated by the work one of them does |
| Gray Market vs. Formal System | Institutional Duty vs. Mercy | A bureaucrat who discovers the gray market is doing what the formal system won't |
| Memory vs. Amnesia | Visibility vs. Erasure | An archive that is weaponized for the wrong side; someone who must erase it |

## How to Use Tensions in Story Development

1. Select a primary tension (the story's spine — the unresolvable conflict)
2. Ask: who is on each pole? What do they want the trade-off to be?
3. Select a secondary tension that makes the primary one harder to navigate (cuts across, not parallel)
4. Find the moment when the character must act — it won't resolve the tension; it will reveal which pole they've chosen
5. Check the ending: is the answer to "was this good or bad?" genuinely uncertain? That uncertainty is the story's argument.
