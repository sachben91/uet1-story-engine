# Tone Rules

**Category:** Craft / Voice
**Source:** `skills/story-protocol/references/tone-card.md`, `skills/story-bible/references/bible.json`

---

## Register

UET-1 stories operate in three registers simultaneously:

1. **Geopolitical thriller** — factions, operations, surveillance infrastructure, jurisdictional chess
2. **Literary fiction** — interiority, character transformation, earned ambiguity
3. **Bureaucratic absurdism** — the system as deadpan comedian; institutional language as moral argument

These are not separate modes. A single sentence can carry all three. A character's bio-band color and their emotional state can appear in the same clause without one undermining the other.

---

## The Mandatory Rules

### 1. No villain enjoying their villainy
Lin is not a villain. He is a man who tried to engineer a free lunch and paid the price. Hale is structural, not theatrical. Corvin Field Services is a contractor. The Fiduciary States believe in what they are doing. The absence of theatrical villainy is what makes the universe's moral argument land.

### 2. No cheap sentiment
Emotion earns itself through indirection. If a character is devastated, the devastation appears in what they notice, what they do, what they fail to do. The Spy says "I know. I know." — that is the moment of grief. It is two words. The reader supplies the rest.

### 3. Surveillance is ambient, not dramatic
ECOROUTE hums. Bio-bands are described like clothing. The Pono is weather. If a surveillance system becomes the dramatic point of a scene, something has gone wrong. The system is always present; its presence is never the news.

### 4. No explanatory dialogue
Characters do not explain the world to each other. Lin does not explain ECOROUTE to Felix. The Pono does not explain Mirage Handler to the reader. What the reader needs to know arrives through what characters notice, react to, or fail to understand.

### 5. The body is always present
If a scene has no physical sensation, something is wrong. What people are eating, the quality of light, the motion of the train, the temperature, the smell — these are not decoration. They are the body's testimony. Lin eats congee with complete attention. That is characterization.

### 6. Humor is dry, procedural, deadpan — never broad
"Order of metaphysical improbability" as an archival browsing mode. The Pono filing "nothing of note" about Murkin's silence. The woman feeding her Pomeranian in the dining car. The children's barricade. The humor is in the gap between institutional language and human reality, not in jokes.

### 7. Price must be paid
Nobody escapes consequence. Rowan's body carries the record. The Spy filed nothing and knows what it cost. Lin got his free lunch and it cost him everything he was protecting. Felix's faith was upgraded, not restored. The price is always proportional and always paid, even when the character doesn't fully understand they've paid it.

### 8. Strategic amnesia is a moral argument, not evasion
"Nothing of note" is not a character failing to act. It is the deliberate use of institutional language to protect someone the institution would otherwise harm. When Murkin files "nothing of note" about the refugee boy, he is not being passive. He is choosing the mercy the institution won't.

## Sentence-Level Guidance

- **Mix sentence lengths deliberately.** Short punches after long flows. Don't let three medium sentences in a row go unbroken.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "She walked to the window" beats "she made her way toward the area near the window."
- **Strong verbs over verb-plus-adverb.** "Stared" beats "looked fixedly."
- **Active over passive** — usually. Passive is correct when the actor is unknown, unimportant, or deliberately withheld.
- **Technical vocabulary reveals character** — it doesn't educate the reader. Felix uses the language of institutional frameworks because that is how he thinks, not because the reader needs to understand the frameworks.

---

## What NOT to Write

- Villains who announce their villainy or enjoy it
- Sentiment that hasn't been earned through prior indirection
- ECOROUTE or bio-bands described as alarming or sinister — they are weather
- Dialogue in which one character explains the world to another for the reader's benefit
- Scenes with no physical sensation (no food, no motion, no light, no temperature)
- Broad humor (comedy based on recognition or exaggeration; nothing that makes you think "comedy scene")
- Characters who escape consequence
- Endings that resolve the tension (the tension is unresolvable; the ending reveals which pole was chosen and what it cost)

## Tonal Calibration by Story Type

| Story type | Dominant register | Tone notes |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Murkin-type | Bureaucratic absurdism primary | Deadpan throughout; the tragedy is in the comedy |
| Rowan-type | Literary primary | Technical vocabulary in service of interiority; body as consciousness |
| Spy-Carrier-type | Literary primary | Restrained; the devastating moment is brief and precise |
| Felix-type | Thriller + Literary intercut | Analytic interiority; irony through juxtaposition; never through comment |

## The UET-1 Moral Framework

The universe does not take sides between its tensions. It dramatizes them. The moral framework is:

1. **Price must be paid.** Consequence is not optional.
2. **Strategic amnesia is the dominant moral act** when institution > justice.
3. **Systems built to liberate and systems built to capture look identical from outside.**
4. **The body is always present.** It records what the mind hasn't processed.
5. **Distance is not safety.** 23,300km is one long room.
6. **The arc of innovation bends toward capture.** Not always. Enough.

These are not stated in the stories. They emerge from what happens.
