# The Toy Box — Rules for Writing in the Trainverse

**Category:** Craft / World Rules
**For:** Anyone writing a story set in the UET-1 universe

This is not a style guide. Style lives in [Tone Rules](tone-rules.md). This is the physical and institutional rulebook — the constraints of the world that stories cannot contradict. Some are absolute. Some are strong defaults with documented exceptions.

---

## The Absolute Rules

### Rule 1 — The train must be moving

Stories take place on the UET-1 while it is in motion. This is the foundational constraint of the trainverse and it is non-negotiable.

The train is not a setting. It is a condition. The motion is what creates the legal fiction the story inhabits — no jurisdiction fully applies, no one can leave, everyone is in the same sealed room. The moment the train stops, the story changes. Stopping is a jurisdictional event (see Rule 4). If a story begins at a station or ends at a station, the train must be moving for the majority of the narrative.

**What this produces:** urgency without a clock. The train is always covering distance. The destination is always approaching. Characters cannot defer their choices indefinitely.

### Rule 2 — ECOROUTE is always running

Every passenger wears a bio-band. ECOROUTE is active throughout the train at all times. There is no scene, no car, no moment aboard UET-1 in which a character is not being tracked. The business car has privacy film on the windows — not privacy from the sensors. The decontamination array in Car 7 is the most intensive read point; the rest of the train is lower-resolution but continuous.

**What this does not mean:** that surveillance is ever the drama. ECOROUTE is weather. Bio-bands are clothes. A character's amber band gets mentioned the same way their coat does — as a physical fact. See [Tone Rules](tone-rules.md) §3.

### Rule 3 — Everyone on board has a bio-band color

Green (compliant), yellow (advisory flag), amber (restricted), deep amber (significant restriction), red (detention pending), black (non-person — system has decided not to see you). A character's band color is part of their physical description. It changes when something happens. It does not announce itself; it is noticed.

A black band has never appeared in a canonical story. It exists in unverified accounts.

### Rule 4 — Stopping is a jurisdictional event

The train's legal protection is motion. When it stops, it becomes subject to the laws of wherever it has stopped. Local authorities can board. Arrests that were not possible at speed become possible. Contraband that was technically in transit becomes discoverable. Characters who were safe in motion may not be safe at a platform.

Gauge change stops (at Dostyk and other border points) are mandatory dwells of 60–90 minutes. These are the most legally exposed moments on the route.

## The Train's Physical World

### The cars

The train has a fixed configuration. Characters can move through it. The key spaces:

| Car | What it is | What it enables |
|-----|-----------|----------------|
| Observation car | Floor-to-ceiling windows, public | Surveillance platform; the most watched and the most watching |
| Dining car | Shared meals, strangers at table | Involuntary proximity; the woman with the Pomeranian |
| Business car | Encrypted comms, privacy-filmed windows | The closest thing to off-record on the train — not actually off-record |
| Premium lounge | Restricted access | Class distinction made physical |
| Car 7 | Gauge-change car, decon array | The most intensive biometric read point; known vulnerability site |
| Casino Car ("Loss and Gain") | Documented; has not yet appeared in a story | Available |

Characters cannot leave the train while it moves. The train is approximately 500 meters long. It takes time to walk from end to end. That distance matters.

### Time and geography are the same thing

At 225 km/h, position is calculable from elapsed travel time. A writer can know where the train is — and what jurisdiction it's approaching — at any point in a story. The route is fixed. Moscow to Astana is 16.5 hours. Almaty to Dostyk is 2 hours and ends in a mandatory stop. This is not background; it is the story's clock.

**The gauge change at Dostyk** is the single most loaded moment on the route: the train stops, Car 7 activates, the decon array reads everyone it can, local jurisdiction applies, and it takes an hour minimum. Any story set on the Central Asian crossing should know whether Dostyk has passed or is coming.

### The full route (west to east)

Lagos → Lisbon → Madrid → Barcelona → Paris → Istanbul → Moscow → Astana → Almaty → Dostyk [gauge change] → Beijing → Shanghai → Guangzhou → Hong Kong → Kunming → Hanoi → Vientiane → Bangkok → Padang Besar → Kuala Lumpur → Woodlands (Singapore)

23,300 km. Approximately 3 days end to end without delays.

## The De-Realized Zones

Two stretches of the route pass through officially recognized De-Realized Zones — territories where standard jurisdictional rendering does not apply. These are documented in official UET-1 infrastructure records, not rumor.

### Caduceus DRZ
**Location:** Belgrade–Sofia corridor (Paris → Istanbul leg)  
**Type:** Pharmaceutical compliance zone  
**Effect on passengers:** Bio-bands rekey on entry and exit. ECOROUTE record for the passage is overwritten on exit — the train's record shows no gap, but the content is replaced.  
**What a writer can do with this:** A conversation in the Caduceus corridor is the closest thing to truly off-record on the train. The Tirzepatide Trail runs through here.

### Zoothesia DRZ
**Location:** ~220 km west of Astana (Moscow → Astana leg)  
**Type:** Perception-management zone (Zoothesia Protocols)  
**Effect on passengers:** Populations outside registered land-use categories are not rendered by the system. The train passes through; the sensor grid continues; certain things are not recorded.  
**Landmark:** The Lights installation marks the western boundary of the Zoothesia DRZ. The Lights is a genuine aesthetic achievement and a surveillance infrastructure. Both are true simultaneously.

## Technology Rules

### What exists and how it behaves

- **Bio-bands** issue compliance color continuously. They cannot be removed without triggering a flag. Tampering is detectable.
- **The Pono** is the institutional memory device used by UET-1 security personnel. It files reports. "Nothing of note" is a legitimate filing. It is also the dominant moral act when mercy requires silence.
- **ECOROUTE retroactive scoring** means that being on the train is not a fixed moment — the record continues to be written after the fact. New information can change a past score.
- **The business car's encrypted comms** are encrypted from external interception, not from ECOROUTE. The distinction matters.
- **Mirage Handler** is a gray-market routing protocol that moves people — and information — through the formal system's blind spots. It uses the train's infrastructure against itself.

### What does not exist

- Real-time communication off the train is unreliable in the Central Asian crossing and certain DRZ passages.
- There is no car that is fully surveillance-free. Privacy film blocks visual observation from outside; it does not block biometric sensors.
- No one boards or deboards while the train is moving.

## The Passenger Population

At capacity: 2,347 passengers. They are in transit across 17 jurisdictions. They include:

- Compliance passengers (green bands, ordinary travelers)
- Gray market operators (Rowan-type; moving things the formal system won't)
- Intelligence personnel with overlapping mandates (Spy-type; their jurisdiction may not be the one the train is currently in)
- People in flight (Murkin-type encounters; they appear at border junctions)
- Faction operatives (Hale-type; operational, deniable)

The ungoverned atmosphere is real: the children's barricade in the corridors, the observation car at 2am, the dining car where strangers share tables. These are not anomalies. They are the train functioning as designed — a space where normal social constraints are loosened but not removed.

**Everyone on board is between places.** That means they are also, in some sense, between selves. The train permits things that the jurisdictions on either end would not.

## Story Constraints Summary

| Constraint | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Setting | Train must be moving for the story's duration |
| Surveillance | ECOROUTE always running; bio-bands on all passengers |
| Jurisdiction | Moving = in none fully; stopped = local law applies |
| Geography | Position is derivable from elapsed time at 225 km/h |
| Gauge change | Mandatory ~75-min stop at Dostyk; Car 7 activates |
| DRZ Caduceus | Bio-band rekey; ECOROUTE record overwritten on exit |
| DRZ Zoothesia | Certain populations and events not rendered by system |
| Car 7 | Most intensive biometric read point on the train |
| Business car | Encrypted from outside; not from ECOROUTE |
| Pono filing | "Nothing of note" is a valid — and moral — act |
| Exits | No one leaves while the train moves |

## What the Train Is, in One Sentence Per Story

- *The Chartered Flesh* — the train is a body that reads bodies.
- *Ghost Inside the Light* — the train is a room where distance does not protect you.
- *Signals in the Margins* — the train is an institution that can be subverted by the people who serve it.
- *The Headless Empire* — the train is the only territory where a stateless system can operate.

Your story gets one more sentence. It must be compatible with the four above.
