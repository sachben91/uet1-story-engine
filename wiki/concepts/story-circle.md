## The Story Circle (Dan Harmon, 8 steps)

The primary structural instrument for UET-1 stories. Every decision — structural, scenic, narrative, vocal — flows from where each character is in their arc at any given moment.

| Step | Label | What it means in UET-1 terms |
|------|-------|------------------------------|
| 1 | **YOU** | A character in a zone of comfort — or the illusion of one. In UET-1: often a professional with a defined role and a working belief system |
| 2 | **NEED** | Something they need — often unknown to them. The NEED is usually the inverse of what they think they want |
| 3 | **GO** | They enter an unfamiliar situation. In UET-1: boarding the train, crossing a border, accepting a contract |
| 4 | **SEARCH** | They adapt and search for what they need. In UET-1: this is the longest section — investigation, observation, small actions |
| 5 | **FIND** | They find what they were looking for — but it comes with a cost they didn't expect |
| 6 | **TAKE** | They take what they found and pay the price |
| 7 | **RETURN** | They return to their familiar world — changed |
| 8 | **CHANGE** | The world is the same. They are different. In UET-1: often the change is ironic — the character becomes more like the thing they were originally resisting |

### Arc Positions in Canon

**Felix Lim** (*The Headless Empire*):
- YOU: Chief of Staff; the arc bends toward justice
- NEED: Confirmation the system works
- GO: The train to Lisbon
- SEARCH: Watching Lin, delivering folders, filing reports
- FIND: The Covenant removes accountability by design; his report was a trade chip
- TAKE: Cynicism as clarity
- RETURN: South on the train, agent of the Covenant
- CHANGE: True believer in the machine's sovereignty — more so than Lin

**Lin Yuan Exact** (*The Headless Empire*):
- YOU: CEO of the Forge; built something unchallengeable
- NEED: Escape without cost — a free lunch
- GO: The train, walking through the gap
- SEARCH: For absolution; making the case to himself
- FIND: No free lunches; the cost is exactly what he was protecting
- TAKE: The world's indifference to elegant plans
- RETURN: The call to Felix
- CHANGE: Settled; commits to the ascetic life; accepts the cost

**Rowan** (*The Chartered Flesh*):
- YOU: Somatic specialist; the body is her document
- NEED: To keep her body sovereign
- GO: Accepts the BRX-90 job
- SEARCH: Delivers the compound; watches the friends
- FIND: Something happened in Car 7 — the Class 2 metabolic variance notification
- TAKE: 30 vials of "real" compound; understanding she didn't understand what she was carrying
- RETURN: Holds the vials
- CHANGE: Ambiguous; the recurring dreams suggest the body holds what the mind hasn't processed

**The Spy** (*Ghost Inside the Light*):
- YOU: Intelligence officer; one month to retirement
- NEED: Some form of connection to his son
- GO: Deploys the drone
- SEARCH: Watches the feed; identifies the compound
- FIND: His son on the tailgate
- TAKE: The choice not to file; silence; "I know. I know."
- RETURN: Recalls the drone
- CHANGE: He chose mercy over duty; whether he understands the full weight of this is ambiguous

**Murkin** (*Signals in the Margins*):
- YOU: Proceduralist; assigned a Pono he didn't want
- NEED: A reason the procedure matters
- GO: Opens the archive out of boredom
- SEARCH: Reads 14,326 documents; follows the soup recipes
- FIND: The system Stepan built; the boy at the siding hut
- TAKE: The knowledge and the choice
- RETURN: Files the report
- CHANGE: A different kind of proceduralist — "nothing of note" as moral act

## MICE Quotient

Four types of story, defined by what must be opened and closed in matched order:

| Type | Opens with | Closes with | UET-1 example |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Milieu** | Character enters a place | Character leaves the place | Felix arriving on the train; leaving Lisbon |
| **Idea** | A question is asked | The question is answered | What is the Covenant actually doing? |
| **Character** | Character is unsatisfied with their world | Character satisfies or accepts their situation | Rowan accepting what her body was used for |
| **Event** | Something disrupts the normal state | Normal state is restored or new normal established | Car 7 attack; Black Wednesday |

**Nesting rule:** MICE threads must close in the reverse order they opened. If a story opens with Milieu then Character, it must close with Character resolution before Milieu resolution. The train journey is always the outermost Milieu thread — it closes when the character leaves.

## Vonnegut Story Shapes (UET-1 templates)

| Shape | Description | UET-1 template |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Man in Hole | Falls into trouble, climbs out changed | Murkin template |
| Cinderella | Rise → crisis → rise → loss — but with change | Felix template: rises with the thesis, loses it, gains something harder |
| Boy Meets Girl | Connection → complication → resolution (or not) | Spy-Carrier template: recognition → silence → the work continues |
| From Bad to Worse | Steady deterioration with no redemption | Not yet used in canon |
| Creation Story | World built from chaos | Lin building the Covenant |

## Structural Templates (from pacing-guide.md)

**Murkin template:** Short, contained, bureaucratic. Single POV. Milieu thread dominant. The discovery is in the archive; the action is in what is not filed. Tone: deadpan.

**Rowan template:** Medium length. Character thread dominant. The body as subject. The cost is invisible until it isn't. Tone: sardonic, technical.

**Spy-Carrier template:** Longer, intercut POVs. Two Character threads running parallel. The connection is the climax; the non-filing is the resolution. Tone: restrained, devastating at one point.

**Felix template:** Longest. Close third-person following one character, with a second character (Lin) whose arc is visible but not narrated. Story Circle as explicit instrument. Tone: dry, analytic, ironic through juxtaposition.
