**Category:** Financial / Legal System; Autonomous Architecture

**Status:** Running without its builder

---

## What It Is

The Covenant is a self-executing contract system embedded in the Strait Country fiscal ledger. It runs autonomously — it does not require human enforcement to function. This was its design. It cannot be modified without the agreement of 24 of 37 keyholders across 19 jurisdictions. Lin Yuan Exact built it and then disappeared. The Covenant continues.

"The contract that requires a human to enforce it has already failed." — Lin Yuan Exact

---

## How It Works

**Architecture:**
- 37 keyholders
- 19 jurisdictions
- Modification threshold: 24/37 keys (supermajority)
- Self-executing: contracts run automatically once conditions are met; no human enforcement required
- Embedded in the Strait Country fiscal ledger: operates within a neutral jurisdictional framework

**Accountability removal by design:** The system removes human judgment from enforcement. This is what Lin built it to do. The same property that makes it impartial makes it incapable of mercy. Systems built to liberate and systems built to capture look identical from the outside.

**Running without its creator:** Lin's disappearance does not affect the Covenant's operation. This was the point. It was designed to be unchallengeable — meaning even by its builder.

---

## Operational Role in *The Headless Empire*

Felix overhears the Covenant's activation mechanics being discussed in the compartment next to Lin's meeting room. He files an institutional report at 2:47am based on what he heard — his last institutional act, his most reasonable one. That report is used as a trade chip in negotiations he didn't know were happening. When Felix arrives in Lisbon, the Strait Country man explains what his report actually did.

Lin used Felix as an instrument to supply the report. The report's use was already architected. Felix's good faith was the point.

---

## The Central Irony

Felix ends the story believing in the Covenant more completely than Lin does. Lin built it to escape. He escaped. The cost was his ability to be in the world consequentially. Felix was used as a tool. He became the machine's true believer. Converts believe harder than founders.
