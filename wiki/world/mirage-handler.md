# Mirage Handler

**Category:** Protocol / Routing System
**Source:** `wiki/sources/Signals in the Margins.pdf`, `skills/story-bible/references/bible.json`
**Origin:** Cold War routing protocol
**Current use:** Refugee movement network (repurposed by Stepan)

---

## What It Is

Mirage Handler is a Cold War-era data routing protocol originally built for moving classified intelligence through institutional archives without detection. Its classified files were eventually replaced with soup recipes (shchi, pelmeni, stewed carp) as decoy filler — the archive developed its own amnesia. The system was never removed: removing it would require 47 vendor libraries and 12 ministerial approvals. It stays in the archive because removal is harder than ignoring it.

Someone — Stepan — repurposed it. The margin symbols in the soup recipes now function as routing codes. Refugees use the symbol system to navigate through gaps in the surveillance grid that Mirage Handler was originally designed to create.

---

## How It Works

**Symbol system:**
- Triangle of dots — routing marker; the first symbol Murkin encounters; the boy at the siding hut carries this card
- Wavy lines — routing marker
- Imperfect circle — routing marker

The symbols appear in the margins of soup recipes within institutional archives. A person who knows the system can find the symbols, read the routing, and navigate accordingly. The infrastructure of disappearance outlives the people it was built to disappear.

**Backward compatibility:** The Pono preserves Mirage Handler files because removing them would require 47 vendor libraries and 12 ministerial approvals. This institutional inertia is what makes the refugee network possible. The system is too old to delete and too woven-in to upgrade.

**Stepan's network:** Stepan (a border runner, now missing) repurposed the margin symbols to give refugees invisible passage. His 14,326 documents in the archive are the infrastructure. The routes are in the margins of the recipes.

---

## The Institutional Amnesia Argument

"God help us all if the world runs on recipes." — Murkin

The soup recipes are not a bug. They are the system protecting itself through strategic amnesia — the archive replaced classified material with recipes because the classified material expired or was too sensitive to keep, but the routing infrastructure couldn't be removed. This creates an archive that has forgotten what it was, still functioning as what it was. Stepan understood this and used it.
