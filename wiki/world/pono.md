# Pono

**Category:** Institutional Memory Device
**Source:** `wiki/sources/UET-1 Wiki.pdf`, `wiki/sources/Signals in the Margins.pdf`, `wiki/sources/Ghost Inside The Light.pdf`, `skills/story-bible/references/bible.json`

---

## What It Is

The Pono is an institutional memory and monitoring device issued to (or imposed on) personnel operating in official capacities on or near the UET-1. It has opinions. It maintains records. It cannot forget — and it files reports containing "nothing of note" as an editorial judgment, not merely a neutral observation.

The UET-1 Wiki PDF describes the experience of having a Pono as: "being followed by a tax collector who has memorised your entire life."

---

## How It Works

The Pono runs as one or more software instances on a device carried by its operator. It monitors, archives, and produces reports. Its capabilities include:

- **Continuous monitoring:** Behavioral and environmental observation
- **Archival browsing:** Access to institutional records, historical files, missing persons databases
- **Report generation:** Produces filed reports; uses language like "nothing of note" as editorial judgment
- **Behavioral scoring:** Can retroactively assess behavior based on new information

**Archival browsing modes (documented in *Signals in the Margins*):**
- Chronological
- Alphabetical
- "Order of metaphysical improbability" — the Pono's own opinion of what matters

---

## Instances (The Spy's configuration)

The Spy runs three Pono instances simultaneously:
- **Clerk** — archival, record-keeping; flags incomplete reports; recipient of "I know. I know."
- **Hacker** — tactical analysis; operational intelligence
- **Agent** — active operations monitoring; manages live deployments

Most operators run a single instance. Three simultaneous instances is unusual and reflects The Spy's deep integration with the device after years in service.

---

## "Nothing of Note" as Moral Act

"Strategic amnesia is the dominant moral act" — the line from the UET-1 universe's moral framework. It applies specifically to the Pono: when an operator files a report that contains "nothing of note," they are making an editorial choice. The institution receives a null record. The mercy is in what is omitted.

This has been used as a deliberate moral act by:
- [Detective Pavel Murkin](../characters/detective-pavel-murkin.md) — filing "nothing of note" for the refugee boy (source: *Signals in the Margins*)
- [The Spy](../characters/the-spy.md) — silencing all three Pono instances rather than filing the drone report (source: *Ghost Inside the Light*)

In both cases, the mercy is only visible if you know what wasn't filed.

## Ambiguities

- The "order of metaphysical improbability" archival mode — is this a standard Pono feature or specific to Murkin's unit?
- Can a Pono be permanently silenced, or only temporarily suppressed? The Spy silences his instances; the story does not specify whether this is recoverable.
- Who manufactures and maintains the Pono? Its institutional affiliation is implied (Fiduciary States adjacent) but not specified.
