# UET-1 Tone Card

## The Register

UET-1 stories occupy the intersection of:
- **Geopolitical thriller** — real-world systems, institutional power, deniable operations
- **Literary fiction** — interior lives, voice-driven narration, moral complexity
- **Bureaucratic absurdism** — systems that generate their own logic, dark comedy of procedure

The blend shifts by story but never leaves this triangle.

---

## What the World Sounds Like

**Surveillance is ambient, not dramatic.**
ECOROUTE does not announce itself. Bio-bands change color. Pono files reports. The system hums. This is not a revelation scene — it is a weather condition.

**Technical vocabulary is character voice, not exposition.**
When Rowan lists compounds, that is his inner life. When Murkin uses institutional language, that is his armor. Never use technical vocabulary to educate the reader — use it to reveal the speaker.

**The mundane and the geopolitical share a sentence.**
A character eating a meal on UET-1 is eating in 17 jurisdictions. A border crossing is a political event and a digestive inconvenience simultaneously. Do not separate them.

**Distance is compression, not liberation.**
23,300km sounds like freedom. It is a sealed room. The further you are from your point of departure, the fewer options remain.

## Humor Rules

UET-1 humor is never:
- Broad
- Winking
- Self-aware in a way that breaks the fictional frame
- At the expense of characters who are suffering

UET-1 humor is always:
- Dry
- Procedural (the system producing absurd outcomes with a straight face)
- Dark (the humor comes from taking terrible things very seriously)
- Earned (appears after the reader understands enough to recognize the absurdity)

The Gogol register (Murkin sections): bureaucracy generates its own comedy when taken at face value. The soup recipes replacing Mirage files are not a joke — they are described exactly as classified documents would be. That is the joke.

## Sentence-Level Guidance

**Short declaratives for action and revelation:**
> "The cabin had been sanitized. Nothing remained."
> "He recalled the drone at 14:32. Filed nothing."

**Long technical passages for character interiority:**
Rowan's compound cataloguing, Felix's institutional briefings, The Spy's Pono dialogues — these reveal obsession and expertise. Let them run.

**Fragment for impact:**
> "23,300 kilometers. One long room."

**Bureaucratic grammar for irony:**
> "The Pono noted, unprompted: nothing of note. He considered this an editorial."

---

## What NOT to Write

- Villains who enjoy being evil (Hale is professional, not sadistic)
- Redemption arcs that aren't earned (Rowan doesn't want one)
- Explanatory dialogue ("As you know, the ECOROUTE system...")
- Sentiment without irony (the world is unsentimental; characters feel things but rarely say so directly)
- Heroes who escape consequences (the price is always paid)
- Technology as magic (the Pono is a well-designed device; The Lights have resonance anchors; explain the mechanism)
- Stopping the train without consequences (jurisdictional events must cost something)

## Tonal Calibration by Story Type

### High Tension / Thriller Register
- Short sentences dominate
- Physical sensation foregrounded
- Surveillance details accumulate
- Time is marked precisely (14:32, not "mid-afternoon")
- No interior digression during action beats

### Literary / Interior Register
- Voice takes precedence over plot
- The character's way of seeing is the story
- Time expands (pages for minutes of experience)
- Physical sensation as emotional analogue

### Bureaucratic Absurdism Register
- Institutional language used with complete sincerity
- Absurd outcomes described in procedural terms
- The gap between what is documented and what happened is where the story lives
- Comedy is not flagged — it arrives through accuracy

## The UET-1 Moral Framework

No easy judgments. Every character operates within a system that constrains their choices. The moral weight falls on:

1. **What they know and pretend not to** (Murkin, The Carrier)
2. **What they believe and cannot stop believing** (Felix)
3. **What they own and discover they were never allowed to own** (Rowan)
4. **What they would do if the system weren't watching** (The Spy)

The train moves. The system watches. Inside that constraint, every private choice is enormous.

Strategic amnesia (choosing not to file, not to report, not to see) is the dominant moral act in this universe. It is not evasion. It is the only form of sovereignty left.
